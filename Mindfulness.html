2. Why the 1.5% Multiplier?

We use a conservative 1.5% "Value-Addition Factor" to ensure financial credibility. This number is derived from three primary research pillars:

The Collective Intelligence Lift (13%)

BCG research shows that mindful teams see a 13% average increase in collective intelligence. We use 1.5% as a conservative estimate of the minimum recovered productivity from reduced "frictional costs" (errors and re-work).

Source: BCG/Awaris Study

The Engagement-Profit Correlation

Leading firms like SAP found that every 1% increase in employee engagement adds roughly €50M-€60M to operating profit. Our formula tracks this "Engagement Lift" created by mindfulness training.

Source: SAP Corporate Impact Data

Retention & Insurance Value

Mindfulness is a statistically significant predictor of lower turnover intentions. The 1.5% accounts for the "Avoided Cost" of replacing burnt-out staff, which typically costs 50%–200% of their salary.

Source: European Journal of Work & Organizational Psych.
